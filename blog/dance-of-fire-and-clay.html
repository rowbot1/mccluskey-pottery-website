The Dance of Fire and Clay

November 15, 2024 • 5 minute read • McCluskey Pottery

Every firing is a conversation between the potter's intention and the kiln's wisdom. In the ancient dance of fire and earth, we find stories written in glaze and ash, tales that speak of transformation and patience, of letting go and embracing the unexpected.

Here in the Limavady studio, as dawn breaks over the Roe Valley, preparations begin for another firing. The kiln, inherited along with the family business, stands ready - a faithful companion that has served multiple generations. Its brick walls have witnessed thousands of transformations, each one unique, each one revealing something new about this ancient craft.

The loading of a kiln is a ritual unto itself. Each piece must find its place, like notes in a symphony. Too close, and they might kiss in the heat, leaving marks of their encounter. Too far apart, and the flame paths won't create those beautiful variations in colour that make each piece sing its own song.

"In the kiln, we don't just fire clay – we release the spirit within it, allowing each piece to find its true voice."

As each vessel is placed carefully on the shelves, consider the journey these pieces have taken. From local clay dug from the banks of the Roe, to the wheel where they found their form, through the patient drying process, and now to this moment of truth. The fire will complete their transformation, turning soft earth into something permanent, something that might outlive us all.

The atmosphere in the kiln is everything. McCluskey Pottery uses a combination of reduction and oxidation firing, techniques passed down through the family line and refined with each generation. The founder perfected these methods, and now the next generation continues to honour them while adding their own discoveries. The way the flame moves through the kiln, the timing of the reduction, the careful management of temperature – these are the variables that turn craft into art.

At 1280 degrees Celsius, magic happens. The glazes begin their dance, melting and flowing, creating patterns that can be guided but never fully controlled. This is the beauty of ceramics – the collaboration between maker and material, between human intention and natural forces.

After thirty-six hours, the firing is complete. But the waiting isn't over. The cooling process is just as critical as the heating. Rush it, and the pieces might crack from thermal shock. So we wait, practicing the patience that pottery demands at every turn.

When the kiln door finally opens, it's like Christmas morning. Each piece has been transformed by the fire, carrying the marks of its journey. Some pieces emerge exactly as envisioned. Others surprise with unexpected colors, textures, or effects. These surprises, these gifts from the kiln gods, are often the most treasured pieces in the collection.

This is the dance of fire and clay – a dance of transformation, patience, and wonder. It's a dance that has been performed here by the family for decades, passed from one generation to the next, yet it never loses its magic. Every firing is a reminder of why this ancient craft continues to inspire joy and wonder, connecting past, present, and future through flame and clay.
